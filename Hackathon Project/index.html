a set of criteria used as a test to evaluate a work of fiction (such as a film) on the basis of its inclusion and representation of female characters
(1) that at least two women are featured
(2) that these women talk to each other
(3) that they discuss something other than a man.

According to Merriam-Webster
Bechdel Test
noun


Alison Bechdel, a cartoonist and illustrator, created the concept of the test within one of her comics released in 1985. She took inspiration from her friend, Liz Wallace, (which is why the test is also known as the Bechdel-Wallace Test) and the works of Virgina Woolf.
